What's the Difference Between a Handyman and a General Contractor in California?

A handyman can legally take on any job where total labor and materials come to $500 or less. Above that dollar amount, or for anything structural, the work legally requires a licensed general contractor. We'll always tell you which category your job falls into before we start — if it needs a licensed contractor, we say so upfront rather than taking the job anyway.
