Signs Your Fencing or Gates Need Attention

Wobbly fence posts, rusting latches, or sagging gates can cause safety risks for pets and children.

Gate Latches Sticking

Gate hardware that has started sticking, misaligning, or failing to latch shut.

Loose Fence Panels

Loose or swaying fence panels that might not survive the next high-wind event.

Rotting Posts

Wooden posts soft near ground contact due to sprinklers.

Pet Containment

Fencing that pets are consistently finding ways to dig under or climb over.

Why Fence Condition Matters More in Simi Valley

Loose fence panels and damaged gate hardware aren't just a nuisance here — during Santa Ana wind season, loose panels are exactly the kind of debris that can catch embers or cause damage in high wind. We check and secure fencing as part of our seasonal wildfire prep recommendations.

Wind Alert

High winds during Santa Ana events put extreme wind load on privacy fences. A single loose panel can act like a sail and tear down adjacent sections.

Can a Handyman Install a Dog Door or Pet-Proof Fencing?

Yes — dog door installs in slider doors and pet-proof fencing add-ons are common, quick jobs that don't require a licensed contractor. Most are done in under an hour.

Boundary Construction Scope

We repair existing fences, gates, and install pet doors. Full property line fencing installations, masonry retaining wall boundary structures, or large chain-link fencing projects generally exceed handyman scope.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you install a dog door in a slider door specifically, not just a standard door?

Yes — slider door dog door installs are one of our regular requests and a straightforward job.

What is cat-proof fencing, and can you add it to my existing fence?

It's an angled or extended barrier added to the top of existing fencing to prevent cats from climbing over — yes, we can typically add this to most existing fence types.

My gate won't latch properly — is that a full gate replacement or a quick fix?

Usually a quick fix — a misaligned latch or worn hinge is far more common than needing a full gate replacement, and we'll check before recommending anything bigger than necessary.

Should I get my fence checked before wind season even if nothing looks obviously broken?

It's worth a quick check — loose panels aren't always obvious until wind actually tests them, and a short inspection before fall is cheaper than a repair after a wind event.
