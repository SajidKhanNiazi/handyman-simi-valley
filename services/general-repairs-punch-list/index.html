Signs Your Punch List Is Overdue

If any of these sound familiar, it's time to bundle your tasks and get them resolved.

Sitting Tasks

You've been meaning to call someone about more than 3 things for over a month.

"Just Dealing With It"

A drawer, door, or cabinet has been broken but tolerated for a while.

Mismatched Hardware

You have mismatched or missing handles, hinges, or knobs in multiple rooms.

Neglected Drywall

Small drywall dings and scuffs have been sitting there untouched since move-in.

Avoiding Spaces

Avoiding parts of your house because something in them isn't working right.

What Counts as a "Punch List" Job

The catch-all category for the small, annoying issues that accumulate over a season.

Doors & Cabinets

Fixing sticking doors, misaligned kitchen cabinets, or loose cupboard doors.

Hinges & Handles

Replacing loose or broken cabinet hinges, drawer glides, and door handles.

Squeaky Floors

Quieting loud, squeaky subfloors, steps, or loose floor boards.

Weatherstripping

Replacing deteriorated weatherstripping and door sweeps to save energy.

Caulk & Grout

Minor caulking and grout touch-ups around showers, tubs, and backsplashes.

Odds-and-Ends

Installing shelving, curtain rods, blinds, towel bars, and mirrors.

Why Bundling Saves Money

Every trade call has some fixed overhead — the drive out, the setup, the initial assessment. If you're paying that overhead five separate times for five separate one-off jobs, you're paying more than you need to. We build punch-list visits specifically to knock out everything on your list in one trip.

Common in Older Simi Valley Homes

Central Simi Valley and Santa Susana both have a lot of homes from the 1960s–70s, and punch lists in these areas tend to run longer — cabinet hardware and hinges from that era wear out, doors settle and stick as the house ages, and small drywall issues accumulate over decades of use.

How Long Does a Typical Punch List Visit Take?

Most punch lists of 5–8 small items take between two and four hours, depending on what's on it. We'll give you a time estimate along with the price estimate before we start, so you know what to expect for your day.

Straightforward Pricing

Punch-list jobs are typically billed hourly rather than per-item, since the mix of small tasks varies so much house to house. See our pricing guide for our hourly rate and minimums.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a minimum number of items for a punch-list visit?

No — even a single small item is a normal call for us. "No job too small" isn't just a slogan here; small standalone jobs make up a large share of what we do.

Can I add items to the list while you're already there?

Usually, yes, if we have time left in the scheduled visit. If the list grows significantly, we'll let you know before continuing so there are no surprises on the invoice.

Do you provide the hardware and materials, or should I buy them?

Either works. If you already have replacement hardware or fixtures, we're happy to install what you provide. Otherwise we'll pick up standard materials and add them to the invoice at cost.

Do you handle punch lists for rental properties or on behalf of a landlord?

Yes — we regularly work directly with landlords or property managers to knock out a tenant's repair list, and can coordinate scheduling directly with the tenant if needed.

What happens if something on the list turns out to be bigger than expected?

We'll stop and tell you before doing any additional work beyond the original estimate — for example, if a "loose hinge" turns out to be a rotted door frame, that's a conversation before it becomes a bigger job, not a surprise on the invoice.
