Signs Your Gutters Need Attention

Look out for sag, overflow, and siding damage to prevent foundation erosion.

Visible Debris

Visible debris buildup (leaves, twigs, pine needles) sitting in the gutter.

Water Overflowing

Water overflowing the gutter edge during rain instead of channeling through downspouts.

Sagging Sections

Sagging or pulling-away gutter sections due to heavy debris weight.

Water Stains

Water stains on the siding or fascia board below a gutter section.

Standing Water

Standing water near the foundation after rain storms.

Why Gutter Cleaning Matters Twice a Year in Simi Valley

Before Santa Ana wind season (fall): Debris-clogged gutters are exactly the kind of fuel that catches embers during high-wind, high-fire-risk conditions. Clear gutters are a genuine home-hardening measure, not just tidiness. See our wildfire prep checklist.

Before wet season (roughly November through March): Nearly all of Simi Valley's annual rain falls in these months. Clogged or damaged gutters during that stretch send water where it shouldn't go — along the fascia, down the siding, or pooling near the foundation — instead of away from the house.

Fire Safety Warning

Wind-blown embers often land in gutters. If dry leaves are present, they ignite instantly, sending fire under the eaves and into the roof framing.

How Often Should Gutters Be Cleaned in Simi Valley?

At minimum, twice a year — once before Santa Ana wind season in the fall and once before the wet season fully sets in. Homes with a lot of nearby trees may need more frequent cleaning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you fix a gutter that's pulling away from the house, or does it need full replacement?

Often a repair — reattaching and resecuring a sagging section is common and usually resolves it. Full replacement is only needed if the gutter material itself is significantly damaged or corroded.

Do you clean gutters on two-story homes?

Yes, we are equipped to safely clean gutters on two-story homes using standard extension ladders and safety equipment.

How do I know if my gutters contributed to a drywall or siding issue?

Water stains directly below a gutter section, especially after a rain event, are a strong sign. If you're dealing with related interior drywall damage, see our drywall repair page.

Is gutter cleaning something I should do myself?

It's doable for some homeowners comfortable on a ladder, but debris buildup can hide sagging sections or damage that's easy to miss from the ground — a professional check periodically catches issues before they become bigger problems.
