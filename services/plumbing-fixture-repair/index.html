Signs You Need a Plumbing Fixture Repair

Catching small leaks and fixture wear early prevents water damage and high utility bills.

Dripping Faucet

A faucet that's been dripping for weeks is almost always a worn cartridge or washer, not a sign of a bigger problem.

Running Toilet

A toilet that runs constantly or needs jiggling to stop, wasting hundreds of gallons of water.

Slow Drainage

Slow drainage at a sink or tub, often indicating an accumulation of soap scum or hair in the P-trap.

Disposal Issues

A garbage disposal that hums but doesn't turn, or has started leaking underneath.

Corrosion & Buildup

Visible corrosion or mineral buildup around a fixture base, showing a slow, persistent leak.

Why Do Faucets and Fixtures Wear Out So Fast in Simi Valley Homes?

Simi Valley's groundwater runs hard, which chews through washers and cartridges faster than homeowners expect. If a faucet or disposal has failed more than once in a few years, hard water — not bad luck — is usually why.

Groundwater Warning

Mineral scaling on faucets can clog aerators and degrade internal gaskets. Installing a scale-reducing filter or swapping to brass-reinforced cartridges can help fixtures last longer in our area.

Should I call a plumber first if the damage is from a leak?

Yes — if you suspect a major main line leak, water heater failure, or slab leak, a licensed plumber is best. For local, under-sink, or fixture leaks, we can diagnose and let you know if a specialist is required.

When You Need a Licensed Plumber Instead

Fixture-level repairs are within handyman scope. New supply lines, sewer line work, water heater replacement, or any job where labor and materials exceed California's $1,000 unlicensed-work cap legally needs a licensed plumber. We'll tell you plainly if that's what your job needs, rather than take it on anyway.

Yes — this is one of our most common calls, usually a flapper or fill valve replacement.
